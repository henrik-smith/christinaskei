## Content Production Guide

### What we need (priority order)

#### 1. Hero Background (most important)
**Type:** Video loop (10-15 sec) OR single hero photo
**What to capture:** Christina coaching/demonstrating in action — NOT posed. Mid-movement, engaged with the work. Could be coaching someone, demonstrating a mobility flow, or leading a session.
**Setting:** Gym floor (Studio Jobbsprek would be ideal — ties into the credibility). Clean background, no clutter.
**Look:** Slightly dark/moody lighting works great — the site overlays a dark filter anyway. Natural light from windows is perfect if available.
**Technical:** Video: landscape/wide, 1920x1080 minimum, MP4. Photo: landscape, minimum 2000px wide. Deliver as `.webp` for web.
**Avoid:** Selfie angle, direct-to-camera smile, overly posed "fitness model" shots. This should feel like someone caught a real moment.

#### 2. About Section Portrait (already have one — but consider an upgrade)
**Type:** Single photo, portrait orientation
**What to capture:** Christina at 3/4 angle or looking slightly off-camera, natural expression. The current gym shot is good — but a second option with **direct eye contact** would increase trust (research-backed).
**Setting:** Gym, natural light. Or simple neutral background (concrete wall, light wood).
**Look:** Warm tones, not overly edited. Should feel approachable but professional.
**Technical:** Portrait orientation, minimum 1200px wide.

#### 3. Christina Intro Video (About section — high conversion impact)
**Type:** 30-60 second talking-head video
**What to say:** Christina speaks directly to camera about:
- Who she helps (not just yogis — anyone who wants a stronger, more mobile body)
- Why she built this program (the gap she saw between hard training and mobility)
- What makes it different (adapted to YOUR level, YOUR schedule)

**Setting:** Gym or clean minimal space. Eye-level camera, good audio is critical (lapel mic or quiet room).
**Look:** Natural, conversational. Not scripted-sounding. One take energy is fine — authenticity > polish.
**Technical:** Vertical (9:16) for social reuse, but capture wide enough to crop to 16:9 for the website embed. 1080p minimum.

#### 4. Video Testimonials (biggest conversion driver)
**Type:** 2-3 short clips (30-60 sec each) from real clients
**Who:** Ideally diverse backgrounds — one gym person, one office worker/parent, one yoga person. Mix of genders.
**What they should say (coach them loosely, don't script):**
- Where they were before ("I never trained" / "I was always stiff" / "I had no routine")
- What changed ("Now I train 3x a week" / "My back pain is gone" / "I feel stronger than ever")
- What surprised them ("How flexible the program is" / "That it actually fits my schedule")

**Setting:** Their home, a gym, or wherever feels natural to them. Casual.
**Technical:** Can be phone-recorded if the audio is clean. Portrait or landscape both work. Good lighting (face a window).

#### 5. Action/Teaching Shots (for Benefits section, general use)
**Type:** 3-5 photos showing variety
**What to capture:**
- Christina demonstrating a strength exercise (not just yoga — think lunges, band work, bodyweight strength)
- Christina demonstrating a mobility/flexibility movement
- Christina coaching someone 1:1 (adjusting form, pointing, engaged)
- A group session moment (even 2-3 people counts)
- Detail shot: hands adjusting, close-up of movement

**Setting:** Gym. Real session if possible.
**Look:** Candid, in-motion. Not "look at camera and smile." The viewer should think "I want to be in that session."
**Technical:** Landscape, minimum 1600px wide.

---

### General Direction

**Do:**
- Shoot in natural or warm light (avoid harsh fluorescent)
- Capture real movement and real coaching moments
- Show diverse body types if possible (not just fitness models)
- Keep backgrounds clean and uncluttered
- Let Christina's energy and warmth come through naturally

**Don't:**
- No heavy filters or Instagram-style color grading
- No stock-photo poses (crossed arms, hands on hips)
- No Gymshark/Nike branding visible (crop or cover logos)
- No mirror selfies
- No pure yoga shots only — mix in strength and mobility work

**Color mood:** Warm, slightly muted. Think Scandinavian editorial, not American fitness marketing. The site palette is dark charcoal + warm white + terracotta — content should feel at home in that world.

The hero video/photo and the testimonial videos will have the biggest impact on conversions. If you can only do one shoot, prioritize those.
